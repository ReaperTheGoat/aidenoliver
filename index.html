Protect Our Water, Protect Our Future

Discover how wasting water affects South Africa and learn simple steps to save it every day.

Why Saving Water Matters

Water is a limited resource. Every drop wasted has a cost on the environment, wildlife, and future generations. South Africa faces droughts and water shortages regularly. By saving water, we conserve life and energy, reduce costs, and protect our planet.

Impact on Agriculture

Water scarcity affects crop production and food supply, leading to higher prices and hunger in some areas.

Energy Costs

Cleaning and pumping water uses energy. Less water use reduces electricity consumption and carbon emissions.

Protect Wildlife

Rivers, lakes, and wetlands provide habitats for animals. Saving water keeps ecosystems balanced.

Daily Water Saving Tips

Shorten Showers

Reduce shower time to save gallons of water every day.

Fix Leaks

A small drip can waste hundreds of litres monthly. Check taps and pipes regularly.

Reuse Water

Collect rainwater or reuse water from washing vegetables for plants.

Full Loads Only

Run dishwashers and washing machines only when full to save water and energy.

Interesting Water Facts

Fact 1

South Africa is one of the driest countries in the world, receiving less rainfall than the global average.

Fact 2

Around 30% of South Africa's population does not have reliable access to clean water.

Fact 3

It takes about 3,000 litres of water to produce 1 kg of meat.

Fact 4

Water-related diseases cause millions of deaths globally every year.

Creative Ways to Save Water

Water-Saving Gardens

Plant native or drought-resistant plants that need less water to thrive.

Greywater Systems

Reuse bath or sink water for irrigation, saving thousands of litres annually.

Rainwater Harvesting

Install rain barrels or tanks to collect rainwater for gardens and cleaning.

Smart Technology

Use sensors, timers, and leak detectors to monitor and save water at home.
