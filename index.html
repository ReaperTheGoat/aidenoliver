Welcome to Teens and Dangers

Being a teenager is exciting but comes with challenges. This website helps you recognize and navigate dangers like online threats, bullying, substance abuse, mental health struggles, and risky behaviors. Click through our sections to learn how to stay safe and make smart choices.

Online Safety

The internet is a powerful tool, but it can expose teens to risks like cyberbullying, scams, and predators.

  • Protect personal information: Never share your address, phone number, or passwords.
  • Use privacy settings on social media to control who sees your posts.
  • Be cautious with strangers online; they may not be who they claim to be.
  • Report cyberbullying or suspicious activity to a trusted adult or platform.

Learn more: StopBullying.gov | FBI Cyber Safety | Test Your Knowledge

Bullying

Bullying can happen in person or online, affecting your confidence and mental health.

  • Tell a trusted adult, like a parent, teacher, or counselor.
  • Document incidents, including screenshots of online harassment.
  • Surround yourself with supportive friends who lift you up.
  • Stand up for others being bullied to create a positive environment.

Help: Call 1-800-273-8255 or visit PACER’s Bullying Prevention | Mental Health Support

Next: Substance Abuse

Substance Abuse

Drugs, alcohol, and vaping can harm your health, relationships, and future.

  • Know the risks: Addiction, legal trouble, and health issues.
  • Resist peer pressure by focusing on healthy hobbies or goals.
  • Seek help if you or someone you know is struggling.
  • Educate yourself about substances to make informed choices.

Resources: NIDA for Teens | SAMHSA Helpline: 1-800-662-HELP | CDC Tobacco Prevention

Next: Mental Health

Mental Health

Stress, anxiety, and depression are common among teens due to school, social media, and life changes.

  • Practice self-care: Exercise, sleep well, and eat healthy.
  • Talk to someone: Friends, family, or a counselor.
  • Limit screen time and engage in real-world activities.
  • Seek professional help if feelings persist or worsen.

Crisis Line: Text HOME to 741741 | NAMI Teen Support | Get Help

Next: Risky Driving

Risky Driving

New drivers face dangers like distracted driving, speeding, and driving under the influence.

  • Always wear a seatbelt and avoid using your phone while driving.
  • Follow speed limits and traffic rules.
  • Never drive after drinking or using drugs.
  • Take a defensive driving course to build skills.

Learn more: NHTSA Teen Driving | TeenSafe
