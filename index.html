Dangers Teens Face Online

The internet can be a wonderful place to learn, socialize, and express yourself — but it also hides serious dangers. Many teens experience bullying online, sometimes called cyberbullying, where hurtful messages, rumors, or embarrassing images are shared to harm or manipulate others.

Even worse, some predators use the internet to manipulate teens for sexual exploitation, pretending to be friends or trustworthy adults. This kind of manipulation is dangerous and can have severe long-term effects on your mental and emotional health.

Remember: Your safety and well-being come first. No one has the right to make you feel scared or uncomfortable online.

How to Recognize Bullying & Manipulation

It’s not always obvious when someone is trying to hurt or manipulate you online. Watch out for these signs:

  • Receiving threatening or mean messages repeatedly
  • Someone pressuring you to share personal info or photos
  • Feeling scared or anxious when using your phone or social media
  • Someone pretending to be your friend but acting secretive or controlling
  • Being asked to keep conversations or relationships secret

What To Do If You Are Targeted

If you or a friend are being bullied or manipulated online, here are some important steps:

  • Tell a trusted adult: Parent, teacher, counselor, or family member
  • Block the person: Use app settings to block or mute the bully
  • Save evidence: Take screenshots or save messages
  • Don’t respond: Engaging may make things worse
  • Report: Use app or website reporting tools
You are not alone. There are people ready to help you.

Get Help & Support (South Africa)

If you feel unsafe or need someone to talk to, reach out to these South African helplines and resources immediately:

  • Childline South Africa: 0800 55 55 55 — Confidential child support and counselling
  • Gender-Based Violence Command Centre: 0800 150 150 — For victims of violence and abuse
  • South African Police Service (SAPS) Emergency: 10111
  • Stop Gender Violence Helpline: 071 123 0784 — SMS and WhatsApp support
  • Child Welfare South Africa: childwelfaresa.org.za

If you feel in immediate danger, call the SAPS emergency number 10111 right away.
